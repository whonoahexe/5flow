## **Label Management: The Hidden Pitfalls in Labeling Workflows**

Label management involves a lot of stakeholders—from legal and regulatory teams to marketing and packaging. With so many people involved, it’s no surprise things fall through the cracks.

Here are the most common causes of labeling and artwork errors that sneak through even after final approval:

### **Last-Minute Changes, No Oversight**

Late-stage edits often bypass formal reviews, especially when teams are racing to meet deadlines.

### **Version Confusion**

Without clear version control, teams may work on outdated or incorrect files—leading to approvals on the wrong version.

### **Regulatory Blind Spots**

Labeling rules evolve constantly. Missing a regulatory update—even by days—can invalidate an otherwise approved label.

### **Low Visibility Across Teams**

When changes are made in isolation, it’s easy for key stakeholders to miss updates—especially without a centralized platform.

### **Siloed Collaboration**

Legal, marketing, design, and compliance teams often work in disconnected systems, leaving room for miscommunication and oversight.
